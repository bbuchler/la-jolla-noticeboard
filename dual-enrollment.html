Altus Dual Enrollment

Pick a course, choose the matching college, and follow the signup steps. Most students should start with Personal Growth at City College or Miramar.

Choose a Course Choose a College Read This First

Why Dual Enrollment?

Free college classes Tuition is waived for dual enrollment students. Other fees are usually handled through school support if they come up.
Real college credit When students pass, they earn college credit from the community college.
Weighted GPA Dual enrollment classes are graded on a 5-point scale, similar to an AP class.
Double high school credit One dual enrollment class earns college credit and two high school credits. For example, one college U.S. History semester can satisfy the full high school U.S. History requirement.
Graduate with a head start Students who start early can finish high school with college classes already completed, sometimes close to a semester of college credit.
Confidence boost Students learn that they can handle a real college class before they graduate high school.

Read This Before You Sign Up

Sign up ASAP

These classes are popular and can fill quickly. If you know you want a course, start the application process now.

Plan for one college class

For most students, one dual enrollment course per semester is the right amount. A second course may be possible only with special approval.

You still have high school work

Dual enrollment means doing both: your college class and your regular Altus high school coursework. Be ready to manage both at the same time.

Best first course: Personal Growth

Career and Life Planning is usually the easiest place to begin because it helps with goals, planning, confidence, and high school credit.

Start here first

San Diego City College

City College is the most familiar path for many students. Use it for Personal Growth or Government.

City Signup Steps

  1. Apply to City College. Create or use your OpenCCC account, then submit the City College application.
  2. Wait for your 10-digit student ID. City says this usually takes 24-48 hours. Check your email and save the confirmation number.
  3. Complete the SDCCD Supplemental Application. This is the special part-time form for high school students. You need your 10-digit student ID before this step.

    Important: Do not use your teacher. Your counselor is Mr. Sutherland: ssutherland@altusschools.net.

  4. Enter your course information exactly. Use the course name, course code, and class number from the card above.
  5. Watch your email. If you do not receive your ID or enrollment updates within a couple of days, tell your teacher.

Second recommended path

San Diego Miramar College

Miramar is a strong option for Personal Growth, U.S. History, and Government. Use it when it fits your course need or when City College fills up.

Miramar Signup Steps

  1. Apply to Miramar College. Create or use your OpenCCC account, then submit the Miramar application.
  2. Wait for your 10-digit student ID. Miramar says this can take about 2 hours. Save or screenshot your ID when it arrives.
  3. Activate your mySDCCD account. Use the email with your student ID to set up your district account.
  4. Complete the SDCCD Supplemental Application. Use your exact course information and include the high school counselor contact.

    Important: Do not use your teacher. Your counselor is Mr. Sutherland: ssutherland@altusschools.net.

  5. Admissions enrolls you manually. Keep checking email and spam. If something stalls, tell your teacher.

More course choices

Coastline College

Coastline is best for students who want more variety. Availability changes, so check the current schedule and talk with your teacher before choosing.

Coastline Signup Steps

  1. Create or use your OpenCCC account. The OpenCCC account is the starting point for Coastline too.
  2. Apply to Coastline College. Coastline says processing usually takes 1-3 business days. Check your email daily for MyCoast login information.
  3. Sign in to MyCoast. Use the account information Coastline sends you.
  4. Complete the High School Release and Parent Consent steps. Do this with teacher guidance. You will need the course name, course number, and counselor information.

    Important: Do not use your teacher. Your counselor is Mr. Sutherland: ssutherland@altusschools.net.

  5. Register for your class in MyCoast. Register early so you do not get waitlisted.

Good starting categories

  • Career and life planning
  • College success
  • Stress management
  • Government
  • Art, music, and world language
  • Cybersecurity, Python, marketing, design, law, and teaching
